<p><p>Sodium Acetate Trihydrate and Sodium Acetate Anhydrous are both chemical compounds derived from the reaction of acetic acid with sodium carbonate or sodium bicarbonate. Sodium Acetate Trihydrate is characterized by having three water molecules associated with each molecule of sodium acetate, making it a hydrated form. It is often used in food preservation, as a buffering agent, and in heating pads. Sodium Acetate Anhydrous, on the other hand, lacks water molecules, making it more concentrated and suitable for applications requiring anhydrous conditions, such as in laboratory settings and certain industrial processes.</p><p>The Sodium Acetate Trihydrate and Sodium Acetate Anhydrous Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.1% during the forecast period.
