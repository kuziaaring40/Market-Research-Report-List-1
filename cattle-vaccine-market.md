<p><p>The cattle vaccine market comprises several types, including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened pathogens that stimulate an immune response without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines, on the other hand, consist of killed pathogens, providing immunity without the risk of infection. Other categories may include subunit, recombinant, and DNA vaccines, each designed to enhance disease resistance in cattle while addressing specific health challenges and improving overall herd productivity and welfare.</p></p>
