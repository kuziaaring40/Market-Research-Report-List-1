<p><p>The Positron Emission Tomography (PET) system market is categorized into three main types: direct imaging, indirect imaging, and alternative imaging. Direct imaging involves using detectors that capture gamma rays emitted from positron annihilation, offering high-resolution images. Indirect imaging relies on scintillation detectors converting gamma rays into visible light, which is then converted to an electronic signal for imaging. Alternative imaging encompasses novel techniques and technologies, such as hybrid imaging systems that combine PET with other modalities like CT or MRI for enhanced diagnostic capabilities.</p></p>
